A narrow and rather long staircase leads from Chiaturi to the monastery, after passing through which it is possible to enter the tunnel. The tunnel is man-made, quite long, and its roof is supported by supporting arches.

The monastery complex consists of many buildings. The main temple is named after the Savior. It is a two-nave basilica, the naves of which are separated from each other by arches resting on massive columns. The facade of the building is decorated with very rich carvings. found here:Carvings around the window, monumental crosses, interlocking rhombuses, cornice carvings, etc. One of the attractions of the Cathedral of the Savior is the wooden kankel, which is completely painted. The door of Kankel is decorated with embossed plates, and in its upper part there is a composition of the Savior and the twelve apostles, various episodes of his (the Savior's) life. In the temple, the wall frescoes are also preserved, including the portraits of the founder of this monastery in the 13th century, Rati Rachi Eristavi, his wife and brother, Niania Kakhaberisdze. Behind the central cathedral is the Mghvimevi cave, where services are held on major holidays.
